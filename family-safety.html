Family Cyber Safety Workshop

Kids, Phones & Online Safety

Practical cybersecurity help for parents raising kids in a connected world.

Reserve a Seat What You’ll Learn

Parents are being asked to manage a digital world they were never trained for.

Smartphones, apps, games, YouTube, messaging, and social media are now part of everyday life for kids. But most parents are left trying to figure out safety settings, online risks, and healthy boundaries on their own.

This workshop is designed to give families simple, practical steps they can actually use at home. No scare tactics. No confusing technical language. Just clear guidance for modern families.

This workshop is for everyday parents, not tech experts.

Whether your child already has a phone or you are preparing for that decision, this class will help you build a safer digital plan for your home.

Workshop Focus

We focus on the areas where kids and families are most likely to run into real-world problems.

  • Smartphones and tablets

What Parents Will Learn

First Phone Readiness

Before handing over the device

Learn what to set up before giving your child a phone, including app approval, screen time limits, privacy settings, location sharing, and account recovery.

iPhone & Android Controls

Simple settings parents can actually use

Understand Apple Screen Time, Google Family Link, app restrictions, content filters, purchase approvals, and device boundaries.

Gaming & App Safety

Roblox, Minecraft, Discord, YouTube, and more

Learn what parents should watch for in games and apps, including chat settings, friend requests, voice chat, scams, strangers, and in-game purchases.

Social Media Awareness

Snapchat, TikTok, Instagram, and messaging apps

Understand privacy settings, disappearing messages, location sharing, group chats, direct messages, and the risks of unknown contacts.

Online Threats

Predators, sextortion, scams, and bullying

Learn how online manipulation happens, what warning signs to watch for, and what to do if your child receives a threat, inappropriate message, or blackmail attempt.

Family Safety Plan

Rules, trust, and conversations

Build healthier expectations around phones, screen time, messaging, bedrooms, app approval, and what your child should do when something feels wrong.

Our Approach

This workshop is not about spying on kids or scaring parents. It is about creating healthy guardrails, better conversations, and age-appropriate independence.

Parental controls matter, but they are only one part of the plan. The goal is to help your child know what to do, who to talk to, and how to respond when something online feels unsafe.
